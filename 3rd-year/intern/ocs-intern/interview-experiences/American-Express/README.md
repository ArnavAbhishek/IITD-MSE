### Test
The test was quite straightforward, covering basic ML and mathematics; no specific preparation was required. However, time management is essential. The more questions you can solve correctly, the higher your chances of securing an interview shortlist.

### Logistics
I secured Day 0 shortlists for both **AmEx** and **BlackRock**. Since the interview slots overlapped, I registered with BlackRock first, informed them of the conflict, and then proceeded to the AmEx interview. You can handle these negotiations easily as company HRs are generally understanding and will ensure you get your interview slot if you are shortlisted.

### Round 1 (Amex Interview)
* The round started with basic HR questions, an introduction, and a resume screening to ensure the content was legitimate and not AI-generated.
* The interviewer then asked the classic AmEx question: "How does AmEx make money?" We then discussed the impact of new tariffs imposed by Trump on India.
* Another question focused on what percentage of revenue AmEx might lose due to these tariffs, which required a case study-based analysis(which I obv didn't prep for).
* For the technical part, I was given a problem that I identified as a clustering task. When asked to implement it, I couldn't recall the specific coding libraries at the moment, so I explained the algorithm mathematically, which is my strong point.
* FYI, I thought I choked the interview until I got the call for the 2nd round.

### Round 2 (Amex Interview)
* This round focused on real-life problems faced by AmEx, specifically modeling consumer behavior from a given dataset, mainly SQL and data analysis tasks.
* I had to explain which model parameters I would choose to build a system for detecting fraudulent transactions and how to verify if a transaction is legitimate.
* The key to this round was "thinking out loud." Even when you are not converging on the perfect answer, keeping the interviewer informed of your thought process is always better; they will often help you, as they are looking for reasons to hire you.

### BlackRock Interview (After the 2nd Amex Round)
* The entire interview centered on my previous research internship.
* They questioned why I wanted to move from a research-heavy background into ML and Analytics, which was a challenging point to justify.
* We discussed my favorite programming languages in a long, exhausting session. By this time, the interviewers also appeared quite frustrated and tired.
