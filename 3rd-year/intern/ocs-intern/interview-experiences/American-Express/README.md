# Internship Interview Experience: 

##American Express
    -Contributed by - Abhishek Arnav

### Test
The Test was really simple(basic ML and math stuff), no specific prep required. Only time management skill is required. The more questions you can solve, more are your chances for a interview shortlist.

### Logistics
I secured Day 0 shortlists for both **AmEx** and **BlackRock**. Since the interview slots overlapped, I registered with BlackRock first, informed them of the conflict, and proceeded to the Amex interview. You can do these negotiations, the company HRs are generally understanding, and they'll make sure you get your interview if you're shortlisted.

### Round 1 (Amex Interview)
* The round started with some basic HR questions, intro and a resume screening, just to ensure that it was not AI-generated
* After which he asked the classic question, every AmEx guy asks, "How does Amex make money?" and then we discussed on the impact of new tariffs imposed by Trump on India.
* Another question was regarding what percentage of revenue Amex might lose due to these tariffs, which required some case study based analysis(which I didn't do, obv).
* Then there was a technical question, I was given a problem that I identified as a clustering task. He asked me to implement it and I couldn't recall the specific coding libraries at the moment, I explained the algorithm, mathematically and ig that was the best part.

### Round 2 (Amex Interview)
* This round focused on real-life problems faced by Amex, specifically modeling consumer behavior from a given dataset.
* I had to explain which model parameters I would choose to build a system for detecting fraudulent transactions and how to verify if a transaction is legitimate.
* The key to this round was "thinking out loud." Even when you are not converging to the perfect answer, keeping the interviewer informed of my thought process is always better, they will help you, as they also want people to hire.

### BlackRock Interview (after the 2nd AmEx round)
* The entire interview centered around my previous research internship.
* They questioned why I wanted to move from a research-heavy background into ML and Analytics, which was a challenging point to justify.
* We discussed my favorite programming languages in a long, exhausting session. By this time, the interviewers also appeared quite frustrated and tired.


### Selection Stats
* Total 18 hires(including SWE and DA roles), 1 MS1, 1 ME2, rest from CS-EE
