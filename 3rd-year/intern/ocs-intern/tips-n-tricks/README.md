**Contributed by Abhishek Arnav**
 - *Intern at American Express, Data Analytics Role*

### Experience
I applied to around 40 companies, primarily focusing on SDE roles while keeping ML as a backup. The test schedule is quite exhausting; I was often taking 7–8 tests a day, returning from classes and staying up until 1:00 or 2:00 a.m. to finish them. You really can't avoid it. Then there's also the obvious branch-based selection that happens at this stage. It is something you just have to face; you might not get a test shortlist even if you have the skillset, simply because of your branch.

### Preparation
I started preparing during my summer break. I had been doing Competitive Programming (CP) since my first year, but I paused it during my third and fourth semesters to focus on boosting my CGPA. Once I resumed, I practiced CP regularly for about 4–5 months. 

Initially, I solved 3–4 problems a day at a minimum. As the internship season approached, I began grinding much harder, solving 10–12 problems daily. Towards the end, when interviews were approaching, I also started practicing on LeetCode to prepare for the medium-hard questions typically asked by major companies.

### Personal Story
The shortlisting process can be very unpredictable. For example, in several tests, I solved every single question perfectly, yet I didn’t get a shortlist. Meanwhile, others who solved fewer questions did get through. It is often hard to tell what the recruiters are thinking. Some companies, like Tier 1-2 Quant and Tier-1 SDEs, tend to be more branch-focused, while others are generally more open to everyone.

Regarding the importance of CGPA: it is very important. I have seen people with fewer technical skills but an 8.8 CGPA crack an interview, while a high-rated 1800 CF programmer might not even get an interview shortlist. Gear up and boost your CGPA as much as possible.

**Moral of the story:** Technical tests are the most critical factor you can control, but the final shortlist involves a high degree of randomness. Maintain your CGPA early so you have the flexibility to focus on the "grind" when the season starts, and don't limit your aim to just the big tech firms.
