Contributed by **Abhishek Arnav**

- Intern at American Express, Data Analytics Role

### Experience
I applied to around 40 companies, primarily focusing on SDE roles while keeping ML as a backup. The schedule is quite exhausting, I was often giving 7–8 tests a day, coming back from classes and staying up until 1:00 or 2:00 a.m. to finish them, you really can't avoid it. Then the obvious branchism that happens at this stage, it's something you just have to face, you might not get a test shortlist, even though you have the skillset just because of the branch.

Regarding the technical reality, the resume honestly doesn't matter as much as people think, the main hurdles are the technical tests and the interviews. If your technical knowledge is solid and you perform well on the tests, the resume often becomes a formality. In my case, I was shortlisted for almost every test(many companies take multiple tests for different roles also), which eventually gave me THREE interview shortlists

### Preparation
I started preparing during my summer break. I had done Competitive Programming (CP) from my first year, but I had to pause it during my third and fourth semesters to focus on boosting my CGPA. Once I resumed, I did CP regularly for about 4–5 months. 

Initially, I solved 3–4 problems a day at minimum. As the internship season approached, I was grinding much harder, solving 10–12 problems daily. Towards the end, when interviews were coming up, I also started practicing on LeetCode to prepare for the medium-hard questions typically asked by companies.

### Personal Story
The shortlisting process can be very unpredictable. For example, in several tests, I solved every single question perfectly, yet I didn’t get a shortlist. Meanwhile, others who solved fewer questions, did get through. It is often hard to tell what the recruiters are thinking. Some companies, like Tier 1-2 Quant and Tier-1 SDEs, tend to be more "branchist," while others are generally more open to everyone.

I chose not to apply for risk management or fintech-specific roles that many others were aiming for, as my main target was always SDE. This intern-season taught me that you can’t rely solely on big names like MAANG, GS, JPMC, etc because your tests can go perfectly and you still might not get the interview.

Coming to the importance of CGPA, it is important(VERY IMPORTANT). I have seen people with no tech skill but a 8.8 CGPA, cracking an interview, while a cracked 1800 CF rated guy not even getting a interview shortlist. So gear up and boost it as much as possible.

**Moral of the story:** Technical tests are the most critical factor you can control, but the final shortlist involves a high degree of randomness. Maintain your CGPA early so you have the flexibility to focus on the "grind" when the season starts, and don't limit your aim to just the BIG tech firms.
